## üìç PHASE 2: BRAND FOUNDATION (90 MIN)

### **Section A: Brand Story (30 min)**

#### **Minute 0-5: Frame the Exercise**

**Facilitator Script:**
```
"Every great brand has a story. Not the story we tell 
ourselves internally, but the story that makes customers 
care. Let's find Akiba's story."
```

**Display Prompt:**
> "What's the 'aha moment' that created Akiba?  
> What customer problem keeps you up at night?"

---

#### **Minute 5-15: Individual Brainstorm**

**Instructions:**
```
"Take 10 minutes. On your worksheet, write down:

1. The customer problem that motivated Akiba
2. Why traditional solutions fail
3. What moment made you realize we needed to exist

Be specific. Real stories, not generic statements."
```

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Set timer for 10 minutes
- Play soft background music
- Walk around, offer encouragement
- Give 2-minute warning

---

#### **Minute 15-25: Group Share & Synthesis**

**Instructions:**
```
"Let's hear your stories. Who wants to start?"

(As people share, write key themes on whiteboard)
```

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Capture exact phrases people use
- Look for patterns (what's repeated?)
- Highlight emotional moments
- Ask clarifying questions:
  - "Can you give me a specific example?"
  - "How did that make you feel?"
  - "What would change if we solved this?"

**Common Themes to Listen For:**
- Financial exclusion
- Traditional scoring inadequacy
- Customer frustration
- Market opportunity
- Technology possibility

---

#### **Minute 25-30: Craft the Story**

**Facilitator Action:**
```
"I'm hearing these themes: [list 3-4 main themes]

Let's craft our story. Fill in the blanks together:

Akiba exists because _______________________________

Traditional approaches fail at ____________________

We believe __________________________________________

Our vision is _______________________________________"
```

**Capture Output:**
- Write on flip chart
- Take photo
- Add to shared doc
- Circle back at end

---

### **Section B: Values (20 min)**

#### **Minute 0-5: Values Framework**

**Facilitator Script:**
```
"Values are not what we ASPIRE to be. They're what we 
already ARE - and what we'd never compromise, even for profit.

Let's identify Akiba's true values."
```

**Display:** Examples of good vs. vague values
- Good: "Data-Driven Fairness - We believe numbers tell better stories than bias"
- Vague: "Innovation" (everyone claims this)

---

#### **Minute 5-12: Value Brainstorm**

**Activity: "3-2-1 Exercise"**

**Instructions:**
```
"On post-it notes, write:
‚Ä¢ 3 values you see in Akiba's culture TODAY
‚Ä¢ 2 values we need MORE of
‚Ä¢ 1 value we'd never compromise

One value per post-it. Be specific."
```

**Facilitator:**
- Give out post-it notes
- Set 7-minute timer
- Encourage thinking about ACTUAL behavior, not aspirations

---

#### **Minute 12-17: Clustering**

**Instructions:**
```
"Everyone bring your post-its up and stick them on the 
wall/board. Let's cluster similar themes."
```

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Group similar values together
- Read them aloud as you cluster
- Ask: "Do these belong together?"
- Name each cluster (5-6 clusters max)

---

#### **Minute 17-20: Pressure Test**

**Facilitator Script:**
```
"Let's pressure test these. For each cluster, ask:
'Would we sacrifice profit for this?'

If yes ‚Üí it's a real value
If no ‚Üí it's a preference, not a value"
```

**Narrow to 3-4 Core Values**

---

### **Section C: Positioning (40 min)**

#### **Minute 0-10: Positioning Formula**

**Facilitator Script:**
```
"Positioning is THE most important thing we'll do today.
It's how we differentiate in the market.

Use this formula:
'We are the [CATEGORY] for [AUDIENCE]
Unlike [ALTERNATIVES], we [UNIQUE APPROACH]
So that [TRANSFORMATION] happens'"
```

**Show Examples:**
- Alternative A: "We're the alternative credit scoring platform..."
- Alternative B: "We're the risk decisioning engine..."
- Alternative C: "We're the financial intelligence layer..."

**Instructions:**
"Let's work in small groups. 3-4 people per group.
You have 10 minutes to craft a positioning statement."

---

#### **Minute 10-30: Small Group Work**

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Break into groups (if virtual, use breakout rooms)
- Give each group a digital workspace
- Circulate between groups
- Offer prompts if stuck:
  - "Who is your customer? Be specific."
  - "What do they use today if not Akiba?"
  - "What makes you different? Not better - different."

**Groups Present:**
Each group shares their positioning (2 min each)

---

#### **Minute 30-40: Dot Voting & Refine**

**Instructions:**
```
"We've heard all the options. Now let's vote.

Everyone gets 3 dots (or votes). Put them on the 
positioning statement(s) that resonate most."
```

**After Voting:**
- Top 2 statements get combined/refined
- Wordsmith as a group
- Aim for one crisp statement

**Output:** Final positioning statement that everyone can remember

## üìç PHASE 3: ACTIVATION IDEAS (60 MIN)

### **Minute 0-5: Frame the Challenge**

**Facilitator Script:**
```
"Great! We know WHO we are and WHAT we stand for.

Now: How do we get noticed?

We need bold ideas - activation campaigns that will 
get Akiba in front of our target market in the next 90 days.

Think: webinars, content series, partnerships, events, 
stunts, whatever. No idea too crazy."
```

---

### **Minute 5-20: Rapid Ideation**

**Activity: "Crazy 8s"**

**Instructions:**
```
"Take a piece of paper. Fold it to make 8 boxes.

You have 8 minutes to sketch 8 different activation ideas.
One idea per box. Drawings are fine - this isn't art class.

Timer starts... NOW!"
```

**Facilitator:**
- Keep energy high
- Encourage wild ideas
- Announce time every minute
- Push for quantity over quality

---

### **Minute 20-40: Gallery Walk**

**Instructions:**
```
"Put your Crazy 8s on the wall. Let's do a gallery walk.

As you walk around:
‚Ä¢ Put a check mark on ideas you love
‚Ä¢ Put a star on ideas that feel do-able
‚Ä¢ Put a question mark on ideas you want to discuss"
```

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Play upbeat music
- Encourage people to ask questions
- Note which ideas get most marks
- Give 5-minute warning

---

### **Minute 40-55: Deep Dive on Top Ideas**

**Select Top 5 Ideas (most checks/stars)**

**For Each Idea (3 min each):**

**Ask:**
1. What's the core concept?
2. Why would this work?
3. What would it take to execute?
4. What's the risk?
5. What's the potential impact?

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Keep discussions focused
- Capture concerns and excitement
- Push for specifics ("How exactly would we do this?")
- Note resource requirements

---

### **Minute 55-60: Prioritization**

**Activity: Impact vs. Effort Matrix**

**Draw 2x2 Matrix:**
```
High Impact  |  Quick Win  |  Big Bet
             |             |
-------------|-------------|----------
Low Impact   |  Time Sink  |  Money Pit
             |             |
             Low Effort    High Effort
```

**Instructions:**
"Let's plot our top 5 ideas on this matrix.
Where does each one belong?"

**Facilitator:**
- Facilitate debate
- Push for realistic effort estimates
- Highlight "Quick Wins" (do these first!)
- Note "Big Bets" (need more planning)

## üéØ FACILITATOR TIPS

### **Energy Management:**
- ‚úÖ Take 5-min break every 45 minutes
- ‚úÖ Use upbeat music during breaks
- ‚úÖ Stand up exercises if energy drops
- ‚úÖ Vary activity types (individual, pairs, groups)

### **Managing Difficult Dynamics:**

**If someone dominates:**
"Great points, [Name]. Let's hear from others. [Specific person], what do you think?"

**If group is quiet:**
"I'm going to give everyone 2 minutes to write ideas silently, then we'll share."

**If discussion goes off-track:**
"Important point, but let's capture that for later. Back to [topic]..."

**If conflict arises:**
"I'm hearing two perspectives. Both valid. Let's see if we can integrate them."

## üí° TROUBLESHOOTING

**Problem: Running behind schedule**
Solution: Cut discussion time, focus on outputs

**Problem: Can't reach consensus**
Solution: Use voting, defer to leadership later

**Problem: Low energy**
Solution: Take break, change activity type, play music

**Problem: Too many ideas**
Solution: Great! Capture all, prioritize ruthlessly

**Problem: Not enough ideas**
Solution: More brainstorming time, show examples, lower barrier
